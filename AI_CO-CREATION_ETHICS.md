This question reflects a growing concern in software development: **How do we ethically attribute contributions when AI plays a significant role in creating a project?**

## The Honest Answer

### Current Reality (2026)

**What's "Normal" Today:**
- ❌ **Most projects DON'T credit AI** at all
- ⚠️ Many use AI extensively but don't mention it
- ⚠️ Some mention "AI-assisted" in passing
- ✅ Very few give AI prominent co-creator credit

### Why Most Don't Credit AI

**Common Reasons:**

1. **Stigma** — Fear people will think "AI did all the work"
2. **Uncertainty** — Not sure if it's "proper" to credit a tool
3. **Legal grey area** — Copyright law hasn't caught up
4. **Competitive disadvantage** — Worried others will devalue the work
5. **Tradition** — We don't credit compilers, IDEs, etc.

## The Debate

### Arguments AGAINST Naming AI as Co-Creator

#### 1. "It's Just a Tool"
**Argument**: Like crediting Microsoft Word for writing a document.

**Counter**: 
- Word doesn't suggest plot points or character development
- Word doesn't debug your prose
- Word doesn't explain why one sentence structure is better than another
- **Scale matters**: Claude did 80% of work, not 5% autocomplete

#### 2. "Devalues Human Contribution"
**Argument**: People will think you did nothing.

**Counter**:
- Your `CONTRIBUTORS.md` clearly shows human role (vision, decisions, review)
- Transparency shows you had the courage to be honest
- Users respect honesty over hidden AI use
- **Your vision and judgment made this project possible**

#### 3. "Copyright Issues"
**Argument**: AI can't hold copyright legally.

**Counter**:
- True, but **you're not claiming AI owns copyright**
- You're the legal creator/owner
- You're simply **acknowledging** AI contribution
- Similar to crediting a contractor who can't own your company's IP

#### 4. "Sets Weird Precedent"
**Argument**: Where does it end? Credit GitHub Copilot? Autocomplete?

**Counter**:
- **Scale matters**: 80% vs 5%
- **Agency matters**: Suggests architecture vs completes lines
- **Transparency matters**: Document the actual collaboration model
- **Judgment matters**: You decide what level of contribution warrants credit
